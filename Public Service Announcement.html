Public Service Announcement

Listen to our public service announcement about HIV prevention and awareness among youth.

*Introduction* :

Hello everyone, my name is Unseba Shoaib, and I'm here to talk about something that affects young people everywhere, knowing your HIV status.

*The Need:*

HIV remains one of the leading public health issues among youth. According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (2023), about one in five new HIV cases in the United States occurs among people aged 13 to 24. Sadly, many young individuals are unaware they are infected, which increases the risk of spreading the virus unknowingly.

*The Solution:*

The good news is that HIV is both preventable and treatable. Testing is simple, confidential, and often free. Visit HIV.gov to find local testing centers or request a self-testing kit to use privately at home. By knowing your status, you take control of your health, seek care if needed, and help protect others.

Affiliation:

This message is brought to you by the University Health Promotion Team, in partnership with the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention.
