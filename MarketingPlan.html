MARKETING PLAN

PH 430: Health Literacy & Communication

Topic: Assessing HIV Status Among High School and College Students

I. Executive Overview

A. Introduction and Purpose

This marketing plan suggests the creation of a youth-focused health communication intervention that will raise HIV status awareness and encourage HIV testing in high school and college students. The intervention is based on the objectives of Healthy People 2030, which are the reduction of new HIV cases and the percentage of adolescents and young adults knowing their HIV status. This initiative comes about through YouthHealth Connect, a governmental agency that is a health communication organization with great experience in working with youths and digital health outreach. YouthHealth Connect is competent in this assignment since it has already established successful school-based health promotion campaigns and has forged cooperation with the community clinics, youth organizations, and college health centers (Wang et al., 2025).

B. Need for the Intervention

Students in high schools and colleges aged between 15 and 24 constitute the priority population of this intervention. The susceptibility to HIV among this age group is high because of early sexual activity, poor use of condoms, poor understanding of the transmission, and low voluntary HIV testing. Due to stigma, fear of being judged, or a lack of confidence, many students do not estimate their own risk or do not get tested. Healthy People 2030 claims that a major part of youths living with HIV are unaware of their status, contributing to further spreading and to the postponement of treatment (Colpani et al., 2024). Other problems that the population is experiencing are a lack of access to youth-oriented health services, poor sex education in the schools, and their dependence on social media, which may be full of misinformation (Asrina et al., 2023). Such a situation brings about a very obvious necessity of intervention that will not only raise the testing rates but also enhance the levels of HIV literacy and decrease the stigma.

C. Description of the Proposed Intervention.

Goals

  • Behavior: Test high school and college students voluntarily more often.
  • Actions: Get students to campus testing events, home tests, and local clinics.
  • Key Influencers: PEs, Campus health centers, school leaders, parents, and social media influencers.
  • Audience: Young people between 15 and 24 years attending high schools, community colleges, and universities.

Objectives

  1. Objective 1 (Short-term): 12 months later, the program will boost HIV knowledge scores by 30% among participants.
  2. Objective 2 (Behavioral): Within the context of the program, 20% more participating students will increase their HIV tests by the end of the program.
  3. Objective 3 (Environmental): Form collaborations with five local clinics to provide free or low-cost youth-friendly testing.

2. Overview of the Project

The work is proposed and referred to as Know Your Status: Youth HIV Prevention campaign that involves digital communications, school-based outreach, and available testing options. Some strategies are social media messaging, educational workshops, peer-led sessions, and mobile testing campuses.

3. Project Outcomes

Short-Term Outcomes (within one year):

  • Increased awareness of HIV prevention and testing
  • Reduced stigma around HIV conversations
  • Higher participation in campus testing events

Long-Term Outcomes (after one year):

  • Sustained increase in routine HIV testing among youth
  • Decrease in undiagnosed HIV cases
  • Improved overall HIV literacy and safer sexual behaviors

II. The Customers (Market Review)

A. Primary Target Audience

The target market is high school and college students, and by analyzing their behavior, culture, demographics, physical considerations, and even psychographics, they can be characterized.

  • On the behavioral level, this group is active in digital communication, has high dependency on social media to inform, and can also be involved in sexual activity. Their willingness to change is different; they are not afraid to get health information, whereas others do not get health information without being compelled by their peers or through school health programs.
  • The group is culturally heterogeneous, as it covers a great variety of ethnic, linguistic, and family backgrounds. There are those students who live in a community where sexual health is openly addressed, and those students whose cultures view such questions as sensitive or not recommended.
  • The demographics of this audience are adolescents and young adults registered in secondary or post-secondary institutions, who work part-time or rely on families or scholarships. They stay in hostels, collectively rented apartments, or with parents, and the level of their educational attainment is between the high school grades and the undergraduate level.
  • Physically, the target population consists of all genders and sexual orientations having different levels of exposure to sexual health risks and access to medical services. However, the lack of healthcare providers or insurance coverage for many students makes school and community-based testing options more important.
  • Psychographically, the audience attaches importance to independence, social affiliation, and personal image. Peer groups and social media influencers, campus culture, and stigma perceptions create an impact on them, aiding their beliefs and behaviors. Most youths think that they are at low risk of getting HIV, thus leading to low laboratory tests. Convenience, privacy, and trust in the source of information influence their decision-making strongly.

B. Secondary Target Audience

The secondary audience will comprise parents, school administrators, teachers, community health workers, and campus health center personnel. These people shape students with their policies, education, and resources. They must assist in the creation of a normal environment of HIV testing where it is accessible and stigma-free.

III. The Product (Product Review)

A. Product Description

It is an intricate HIV education and awareness program for the youth. It also includes the factual workshops, social media messages, on-campus testing mobile events, and a partnership with nearby clinics. The product offers interactive and trustworthy facts about HIV, testing directions, which are available, and continuous support through the internet platforms. Such a campaign is also peculiar, because this is chosen with a specific target at high school and college students, incorporating the communication methods that are interesting in their media consumption preferences, such as Instagram reels, TikTok videos, and short-form educational media (Chiou et al., 2021).

B. Product Distinctiveness.

The strengths of the program include the fact that it is very youth-oriented, it has a relatable program, and it respects confidentiality. The campaign will integrate peer educators who will have the role of aiding information in such an approach, which is relatable, less stigmatizing, and will help form trust. Also, the proliferation of mobile testing units on campuses will raise the level of accessibility and convenience, promoting the use of HIV screening (Mikołajczak-Degrauwe et al., 2023).

IV. Strategies

Position Description
Position The image that is desired on the campaign should be the one that shows that HIV testing is a normal and responsible action and that it empowers people, instead of an action that is accompanied by fear or shame. It is the unique feature of the intervention, the communication style based on the youth and its incorporation into the school settings. These are sensational selling points that the project will help them stand out among the conventional clinical techniques.
Product The main attributes highlighted are the free-testing events, informational support through digital materials, peer educators, and confidential services. The product meets an important requirement to remove obstacles to testing and close the knowledge gaps between students.
Price The students are free of the program. The funding origin is the grants, community alliances, and school health funds. It has value due to the high health benefits, as it offers students a chance to make informed decisions, alleviates anxiety, and averts health issues through early diagnosis.
Promotion The campaign messages will be empowerment-based, confidential, and accessible. The marketing process will be implemented through school emails, youth-focused radio stations, campus magazines, and social media, such as Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube.

B. Justification

These spending expenses are justified because all the factors directly relate to the provision of programs and student engagement. The idea will be popularized with the help of advertisements, will be made relatable by peer teachers, and will be made accessible through mobile testing. The combination of all these factors is what results in a comprehensive and successful intervention that will result in a transformation of the youth to a profound level.
